FAQ

Common Questions About Psychology

What is attachment theory?

Attachment theory explains how early relationships with caregivers shape our adult relationship patterns. There are four main attachment styles: secure, anxious, avoidant, and fearful-avoidant.

Why do I seek validation from others?

Validation seeking is a natural human behavior rooted in our need for social acceptance. However, excessive external validation can indicate low self-worth and emotional dependency.

What is dark psychology?

Dark psychology refers to the study of manipulative tactics used by some people to control others. Understanding these patterns helps protect against gaslighting, narcissism, and emotional abuse.

How does dopamine affect behavior?

Dopamine is a neurotransmitter that drives reward-seeking behavior. Modern life creates constant dopamine loops through social media, notifications, and instant gratification, affecting motivation and focus.

What is stoicism?

Stoicism is a philosophy that teaches mental strength through focusing on what you can control and accepting what you cannot. It provides practical tools for emotional resilience in difficult situations.

How can I change my attachment style?

While attachment styles are formed early, they can evolve through conscious effort, therapy, and secure relationship experiences. The process involves self-awareness, boundary work, and gradually building trust in healthy relationships.
